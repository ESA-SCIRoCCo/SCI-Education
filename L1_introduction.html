What do we mean by
Soil Moisture?

Soil consists of three phases:

  • solid phase (mineral and organic matter)
  • liquid phase (water)
  • gaseous phase (air)

Soil Moisture

Water content of soil, expressed in
volumetric units

$$\theta = \frac{Water\;Volume\;(m^{3})}{Total\;Volume\;(m^{3})}$$
or in gravimetric units

$$u = \frac{Mass\;of\;Water\;(kg)}{Mass\;of\;dry\;soil\;(kg)}$$

Further Definitions:

Volumetric water content $\theta$:
$$\theta = \frac{V_{W}}{V_{T}}$$
Gravimetric water content $u$:
$$u = \frac{M_{W}}{M_{S}}$$
Degree of Saturation $S$:
$$S = \frac{V_{W}}{V_{V}} = \frac{V_{W}}{V_{T} \phi} = \frac{\theta}{\phi}$$


Porosity $\phi$:
$\phi = \frac{V_{V}}{V_{T}}$
($V_{V}$...pore space)

    Why do we use Microwaves?

    • Microwaves: 1 mm - 1 m
    • Band designations

    Source: PPP 1 Microwave Remote Sensing #18

    • Advantages compared to visible/IR range
      • microwaves penetrate the atmosphere even if there's rain or clouds
      • independent of the sun as source of illumination
      • deeper penetration into vegetation and soil

    Active and Passive Microwave sensors

    • Active
      • Active remote sensors send out their own electromagnetic energy and measure what comes back from the Earth's surface
      • Sensors
        • Altimeters
        • Side-looking real aperture radar
        • Scatterometer (SCAT)
        • Synthetic Aperture Radar (SAR)

    • Passive
      • Passive remote sensing systems record electromagnetic energy that is reflected or emitted from the Earth surface
      • Sensors
        • Microwave radiometers
