What do we mean by
Soil Moisture?

Soil consists of three phases:

  • solid phase (mineral and organic matter)
  • liquid phase (water)
  • gaseous phase (air)

Soil Moisture

Water content of soil, expressed in
volumetric units

$$\theta = \frac{Water\;Volume\;(m^{3})}{Total\;Volume\;(m^{3})}$$
or in gravimetric units

$$u = \frac{Mass\;of\;Water\;(kg)}{Mass\;of\;dry\;soil\;(kg)}$$

Further Definitions:

Volumetric water content $\theta$:
$$\theta = \frac{V_{W}}{V_{T}}$$
Gravimetric water content $u$:
$$u = \frac{M_{W}}{M_{S}}$$
Degree of Saturation $S$:
$$S = \frac{V_{W}}{V_{V}} = \frac{V_{W}}{V_{T} \phi} = \frac{\theta}{\phi}$$


where $\phi = V_{V} / V_{T}$ is the porosity and $V_{V}$ is referred to as pore space

Why is Soil Moisture so crucial?

Earth Water Distribution

Total volume of soil moisture: 16 500 km3 (0.0012%)

The Earth's Water Cycle

Important component of Water Cycle

Influences hydrological and agricultural processes

  • Runoff potential and Flood control
  • Drought development
  • Water available to plants

    • Land / Atmosphere interaction

    Regulating heat energy exchange through:

  • Evaporation
  • Transpiration

    • Development of weather patterns and the production of precipitation
